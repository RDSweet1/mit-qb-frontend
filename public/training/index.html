2. Time Entries

▶

Where does the data come from?

Field technicians clock in and out using the QuickBooks Workforce mobile app. This data syncs to QuickBooks Online and is pulled into our system automatically.

Viewing entries

Use the customer filter dropdown at the top to narrow entries to a single customer. When set to All Customers, entries are grouped by customer. Selecting a specific customer switches to a flat list for that customer only. Use the This Week / Last Week preset buttons or the date pickers to change the date range.

Editing notes

  1. Find the time entry you want to edit
  2. Click the pencil icon next to the notes field
  3. Type your changes in the text area
  4. Click Save
What happens when you save? Edited notes are written back to QuickBooks Online (the billing system). The entry is marked as Manually Edited and will not be overwritten by future syncs.

Locking & Unlocking

Entries are locked by default to prevent accidental changes. To edit:

  1. Click the lock icon on the entry to unlock it
  2. Make your edits
  3. Click the lock icon again to re-lock when finished
Permissions You need the can_edit_time permission or admin access to unlock entries. If the lock icon is grayed out, ask an admin to grant you access.

Status badges

BadgeMeaning
UnbilledEntry not yet included in a report
Report SentIncluded in a report sent to the customer
SupplementalChanged after initial report; supplemental report sent
AcceptedCustomer accepted the report
DisputedCustomer disputed the report

Requesting clarification

If a time entry needs clarification from the field tech:

  1. Click the Clarify button on the entry (or select multiple and click Clarify in the action bar)
  2. Type your question and assign it to the technician
  3. The tech receives an email with a link to respond
  4. Their response appears in the Clarifications tab

3. Reports & Reminders

▶

Weekly reports

The Reports page shows one card per customer per week. Each card displays:

  • Customer name and week date range
  • Total hours and number of entries
  • Current status (Pending, Sent, Accepted, Disputed)

Sending reports

  1. Navigate to the Reports tab
  2. Select the week using the date picker
  3. Click Send Reminder for individual customers, or use the batch send button
  4. The customer receives an email with a summary and a "Review & Accept" button
Follow-up reminders If a customer hasn't responded within 3 days, the system automatically sends follow-up reminders (up to 3). This runs on weekdays at 9 AM.

Supplemental reports

If you edit time entries after a report has been sent, the changes are tracked. You can then send a Supplemental Report that highlights only what changed.

Report statuses

StatusMeaning
PendingReport not yet sent
SentReport emailed to customer
Supplemental SentUpdated report sent after edits
AcceptedCustomer approved the report
DisputedCustomer flagged an issue
No TimeNo billable time for this customer/week

Auto-accept

Reports that go 14 days without a response are automatically accepted. This runs at 5 PM on weekdays.

Internal MIT reports MIT Consulting's own time entries are included in all reporting flows — daily reports, weekly roll-ups, follow-up reminders, and auto-accept. Internal reports are sent to accounting@mitigationconsulting.com. Every report email displays the customer/project name prominently in the subject line and email header.

4. Customer Review Portal

▶

How it works (customer's perspective)

  1. Customer receives an email with a time activity summary
  2. They click "Review & Accept" to open the review portal
  3. The portal shows all time entries for that week with details
  4. Customer clicks Accept or Dispute
No login required The review portal uses secure UUID tokens. Customers don't need an account — they just click the link in their email.

What happens after acceptance?

  • The report status changes to Accepted
  • Sharon receives a confirmation email
  • The entries can now be invoiced

What happens after a dispute?

  • The report status changes to Disputed
  • Sharon receives a notification with the customer's comments
  • The issue should be resolved and a supplemental report sent if needed

5. Invoices

▶

Previewing invoices

  1. Go to the Invoices tab
  2. Select the week and customer(s)
  3. Click Preview to see a draft invoice with line items
  4. Review the service items, hours, and rates

Generating invoices in QuickBooks

  1. After previewing, click Generate Invoice
  2. The system creates the invoice directly in QuickBooks Online
  3. Time entries are marked as Invoiced
Invoiced entries are locked Once an entry is invoiced, it cannot be unlocked or edited. If corrections are needed, void the invoice in QB first.

Service items & rates

Invoice line items come from service items synced from QuickBooks. Each service item has a rate that determines the line item amount. Manage rates in Settings > Service Items.

6. Accounts Receivable

▶

What is the AR module?

The Accounts Receivable module automates the collection sequence for overdue invoices. It tracks every open invoice from creation through payment (or attorney referral), sends dunning emails on a configurable schedule, and records all interactions — emails, calls, promises, disputes — in a single timeline per invoice.

The four sub-tabs

Sub-tabPurpose
DashboardAging overview with buckets (1–15, 16–30, 31–60, 60+ days), KPI cards, and alert banners for disputes and attorney referrals
InvoicesSearchable list of all open invoices with status badges and aging. Click any row to open the detail drawer showing the full activity timeline.
Queue“Due Today” and “Upcoming” sections — the best starting point for daily AR work
ActivitySearchable log of every interaction across all invoices — emails sent, customer replies, calls, notes, payments, and disputes

Automated dunning sequence

Once an invoice is created, the system progresses through six stages over 60 days:

StageTimingAction
0 — Invoice SentDay 0Invoice created and emailed
1 — Friendly Reminder+7 daysAutomated reminder email
2 — Grace Expired+15 daysFirmer notice; billing hold applied
3 — Second Notice+30 daysEscalated collection email
4 — Final Notice+45 daysFinal warning before legal action
5 — Attorney+60 daysInternal alert only — no email sent; requires manual decision
Stages 1–4 fire automatically at 9 AM each day. Dunning pauses automatically when a customer sets a promise-to-pay date or when an invoice is disputed.

Sync buttons

Three sync buttons appear in the top-right corner of the AR page:

  • Sync from QB — pulls all open invoices from QuickBooks, adds new ones, and marks paid ones as resolved
  • Sync Payments — updates balance-due figures from QuickBooks (runs automatically each morning)
  • Sync Emails — scans the accounting@ inbox and sent folder (last 90 days) to capture customer replies and manual Outlook emails
Detailed guide available For step-by-step instructions on working invoices, logging calls, handling disputes, and using the detail drawer, see the full Accounts Receivable Guide →

7. Monitoring & Automation

▶

Admin > Scheduling tab

All automations (send-reminder, follow-up, auto-accept, reconciliation, AR dunning, profitability, etc.) can be paused and resumed from the Admin > Scheduling tab. You can also change the day and time each automation runs. Paused automations show a yellow badge.

Health Digest

Every morning at 7 AM, a summary email is sent to administrators listing the status of every automation:

StatusMeaning
OKFunction ran successfully within its expected window
STALEFunction has not run in longer than expected — may need attention
ERRORFunction’s last run failed
PAUSEDAutomation is intentionally paused via the Scheduling tab

Self-Heal

A repair loop runs every 15 minutes. It performs two checks:

  • Inbox scan: looks for ALERT emails in the accounting@ inbox indicating a function failure, then retries the failed function
  • Schedule check: compares each function’s last run time against its expected schedule in schedule_config and retries any that are overdue

After each repair attempt, the system sends either a resolution email (if the retry succeeded) or an escalation email (if it failed again).

Watchdog Alerts

When an edge function fails for the first time, a watchdog alert email is sent immediately to administrators. This provides early warning before the next Health Digest or self-heal cycle.

Mid-Week Oversight

Every Wednesday, the system checks for time entries added or modified since the last weekly report was sent. If any customers have new entries that would require a supplemental report, an oversight email is sent to Sharon listing the affected customers and entry counts so they can be addressed before the end of the week.

No action needed most days The monitoring layer is fully automated. You only need to intervene when a Health Digest shows ERROR or STALE status, or when you receive a watchdog alert.

9. Troubleshooting

▶

Common issues

ProblemLikely causeSolution
Can't sign in Not a MIT Microsoft 365 account Use your @mitigationconsulting.com email. Personal Microsoft accounts won't work.
Time entries look stale QB sync hasn't run recently Click the Sync button on the Time Entries page to trigger a manual sync.
Edits don't save Entry is locked, or missing permissions Unlock the entry first. If lock icon is grayed out, ask an admin for can_edit_time access.
Changes revert after saving RLS policy issue Contact the system administrator. The database may need an RLS policy update.
Customer didn't get email Email went to spam, or wrong contact email Check the customer's contact email in QuickBooks. Ask them to check spam/junk folders.
Invoice generation fails QB token expired Contact the system administrator to refresh the QuickBooks Online token.
Lock icon grayed out Insufficient permissions Ask an admin to grant you can_edit_time permission.
Automation not running Automation is paused Check Settings page. Look for yellow "Paused" badges and resume the automation.

Getting help

Contact For technical issues, contact the system administrator. For billing/process questions, contact Sharon.
