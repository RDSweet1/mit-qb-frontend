Why this exists

Every time the platform sends a client an invoice, a document share, a project invite, or a delta-comparison report, there's a chance the email doesn't deliver. The address might be wrong, their mailbox might be full, the domain might have changed, etc. Microsoft Outlook sends back an "Undeliverable:" notification (called an NDR — non-delivery report) when this happens.

Until 2026-04-26 those NDRs piled up in accounting@'s inbox unread. The result: a client would say "we never got your invoice" and we'd have no way to know that — let alone fix it.

The bottom line Every undelivered email is a client who didn't get their document. We need every single one tracked and cleared. That's what this screen is for.
2

How you'll know there's a bounce to clear

Two places will alert you:

WhereWhat you see
Home dashboard card The Email Bounces tile (alongside Time Entries, Clarifications, Weekly Reports, etc.) will show a red "N open" badge in the top-right when there's something to clear. Click the tile to go to the management screen.
Floating chip — every page A small red banner appears in the bottom-right corner that says "N email bounces need follow-up · Click to review & clear". It animate-pulses gently to catch your eye. Click it to go to the management screen. The chip hides itself once the queue is empty (or when you're already on the Bounces screen).
The bot scans every 15 minutes New bounces will appear automatically without you doing anything. There's no "refresh inbox" button to push.
3

The Bounces screen

The screen lives at /bounces. It has three tabs at the top:

  • Open — the queue of bounces that still need your attention. This is where you spend your time.
  • All — every bounce ever recorded, regardless of status.
  • Resolved — history of bounces you've already cleared. Useful if a client asks "did you actually resend that invoice?" — you can look it up.

Each bounce shows a category badge (which kind of email failed):

Invoice Document Share Project Invite Report Other

…plus the original subject, the address that bounced, an invoice number (if we could detect one), the SMTP error code, the plain-English reason, and an expandable view of the raw bounce body for context.

Each open bounce gives you two buttons on the right:

  • Resolve… — opens the action panel below the row. Use this when you actually want to do something about the bounce.
  • Mark seen — quick acknowledgement that you've looked at it. Stays in the queue but stops being "new." Useful if you want to come back to it later.
4

Resending to a corrected address

This is the main path. The original recipient was wrong; you have the right one.

StepWhat to do
1Click Resolve… on the bounce row.
2Confirm the Resend to corrected address tab is selected (default).
3In Send to, type the corrected email address. This is required.
4The Subject field is pre-filled with the original. Edit it only if you need to.
5The Body field is optional. Leave it blank and we'll send a clean generic message that says "we previously tried to send you [original subject] regarding invoice #X but the delivery failed; we're resending now". Or write your own.
6Click Send & Resolve (green button). The email goes out from accounting@mitigationconsulting.com via Outlook. The bounce row is automatically marked resolved with a record of where it went.
Where the email comes from Resends always send from accounting@mitigationconsulting.com. The recipient sees a normal email from accounting; they don't see the bounce-tracker UI or know the original failed.
What if you also need to update the customer's email in QuickBooks? Resend first to get the document delivered. Then go to Customer Recipients (in the main nav) and update the saved address so future invoices go to the right place automatically. Or use the "I handled this manually" path below if you'd rather do it all in one motion.
5

Marking a bounce resolved without resending

Sometimes the right action isn't "resend from this screen" — maybe you already called the client, already updated their email in QB, or the bounce is for a stale test that doesn't need any follow-up. Use this path for those.

StepWhat to do
1Click Resolve… on the bounce row.
2Click the I handled this manually tab.
3Pick what you actually did from the dropdown:
  • Updated customer email in QuickBooks / Customer Recipients — you fixed the saved address; future emails will be correct.
  • Called client directly — out-of-band resolution, document delivered another way.
  • No action needed — intentional bounce, stale test, or otherwise not worth chasing.
  • Other — explain what you did in the notes field.
4(Optional) Add a one-line note in Notes. Anything memorable — e.g., "Used Premier-Omnisense backup contact, will email Sue Monday."
5Click Mark resolved. The row leaves the open queue and shows up under Resolved with your notes attached.
6

Looking up resolved bounces

Click the Resolved tab to see every bounce you've cleared. Each row shows:

  • The action you picked (Resent / Updated email / Called / etc.)
  • Who resolved it (your email)
  • When it was resolved
  • Notes you added
  • For resends: which address it went to and the Outlook message id (proof the email actually sent)
Audit trail Anything resolved here is logged forever in the database. If a client says "you never sent that invoice," you can pull up the resolved row and prove you did, with the Outlook message id from when you sent it.

Walkthrough — first time you use this

Recommended path the first time you sit down with the screen:

  1. Open the timesheet app like normal.
  2. If there are open bounces, the red Email Bounces card on the home dashboard will have a count. Click it.
  3. Pick a bounce that looks easy — a Test Project or a clearly-bad email address — and click Resolve… → I handled this manually → No action needed → Mark resolved. Watch it leave the queue.
  4. Pick a real one. Click Resolve… → Send to the corrected address → Send & Resolve. Watch the success toast.
  5. Switch to the Resolved tab. Confirm both rows are there with your name + the action you picked.
  6. Switch back to Open. The count drops. The floating red chip in the bottom-right disappears once the queue is empty.

You should now be able to

  1. Spot a new bounce within 15 minutes of it arriving (dashboard card + floating chip).
  2. Resend a bounced email to a corrected address from the Bounces screen.
  3. Mark a bounce resolved without resending, with a note explaining what you actually did.
  4. Look up a previously-resolved bounce to prove a document was delivered.
