1. Overview — What is Net Position?

▶

Net Position gives you a single number that answers the question: "If we collected everything owed to us and paid everything we owe, how much cash would we have?"

Cash on Hand − CC Debt + Accounts Receivable − Accounts Payable = Net Position

Where to find it

  1. Click Profitability in the top navigation bar
  2. Click the Cash Position tab
  3. Click "Refresh Live Balances" (the purple button) to pull live data from QuickBooks

Data sources

DataSourceUpdated
Bank balancesQuickBooks Online (live)Each time you click Refresh
Credit card balancesQuickBooks Online (live)Each time you click Refresh
Accounts ReceivableSupabase (synced invoices)Last payment sync
Accounts PayableQuickBooks Online (live)Each time you click Refresh
CC Expense categoriesSupabase (daily review)Last Daily Review sync
Payment safety Credit card payments (transfers from bank to CC) are not counted as expenses. The CC balance from QuickBooks already reflects all payments applied — it is the single source of truth for what you owe.

2. Getting Live Balances

▶

Two buttons in the sync panel

The Cash Position tab has two separate sync buttons:

ButtonColorWhat it does
Refresh Live Balances Purple Pulls live bank/CC balances and open bills from QB, calculates net position
Sync Payments Blue Syncs payment history, deposits, and invoice balances for the YTD section below

How to refresh

  1. Navigate to Profitability > Cash Position
  2. Click the purple "Refresh Live Balances" button
  3. Wait for the loading to complete (this queries QuickBooks live, may take a few seconds)
  4. The Net Position cards, Account Balances, CC Breakdown, and Bills sections will appear
First time loading The net position sections only appear after you click "Refresh Live Balances." Before clicking, you'll only see the YTD summary cards and weekly detail table.

After refreshing, a timestamp appears below the cards showing when the data was fetched (e.g., "Live balances as of Feb 25, 10:30 AM").

3. Net Position Summary Cards

▶

After refreshing, 5 large cards appear at the top of the page:

CardColorWhat it shows
Cash on Hand Green Total balance across all bank accounts
CC Outstanding Purple/Red Total balance owed on all credit cards
Receivables (A/R) Blue Total unpaid invoices — money customers owe you
Payables (A/P) Amber Total unpaid bills — money you owe vendors
Net Position Green or Red The bottom line: green = positive, red = negative
Reading the Net Position card A green Net Position means you have more assets than liabilities. A red Net Position means liabilities exceed your liquid assets. The formula is shown underneath: "Cash − CC Debt + A/R − A/P."

The A/R and A/P cards also show a count (e.g., "37 invoices" or "2 bills") so you can quickly see how many open items there are.

🏦

4. Account Balances

▶

Below the summary cards is the Account Balances table. It shows every active bank and credit card account from QuickBooks with their current balance.

What you'll see

  • Bank accounts — shown first, with balances in green
  • Credit Card accounts — shown second, with balances in red (amount owed)
  • Total Bank and Total CC Debt rows at the bottom

Collapsing/expanding

Click the "Account Balances" header to collapse or expand this section. It is expanded by default after refreshing.

Balance meaning differs by type For bank accounts, a positive balance = money you have. For credit cards, a positive balance = money you owe. The CC balance already includes all payments QB has processed — if you paid off a card today, the balance would show $0 on the next refresh.
💳

5. Credit Card Expense Breakdown

▶

This section answers "Where is the CC money going?" — it breaks down charges on each credit card by expense category.

How to view

  1. After refreshing live balances, find the "CC Expense Breakdown" section
  2. Click the header to expand it
  3. Each credit card account gets its own sub-section with a table of categories

What the columns mean

ColumnDescription
CategoryExpense category (e.g., "Internet/Phone", "Office Supplies", "Uncategorized")
AmountTotal dollar amount for that category
TxnsNumber of individual transactions in that category
Uncategorized expenses Rows labeled "Uncategorized" are highlighted in amber. These are charges that haven't been categorized yet in the Daily Review. To categorize them, go to the Daily Review page and assign categories.

Empty state

If no CC expense data appears, you'll see the message: "Run Daily Review Sync to populate CC expense data." This means the Daily Review sync hasn't pulled CC Purchase transactions yet.

Only actual charges This breakdown only counts Purchase transactions (actual CC charges). It does not include Transfers (CC payments from bank) or BillPayments — so there's no double-counting.
📄

6. Upcoming Bills / Accounts Payable

▶

This section shows all open bills from QuickBooks — money you owe to vendors that hasn't been paid yet.

How to view

  1. After refreshing live balances, find the "Upcoming Bills / A/P" section
  2. Click the header to expand it
  3. Bills are sorted by due date (soonest first)

Table columns

ColumnDescription
VendorWho you owe
Due DateWhen payment is due
TotalOriginal bill amount
BalanceRemaining amount to pay (after partial payments)
StatusColor-coded badge
DaysDays until due (or "Xd overdue")

Status badges

BadgeMeaning
Overdue Past due date — row highlighted in red
Due Soon Due within 7 days
Current More than 7 days until due
Overdue bills Overdue bills are highlighted with a red background. Pay attention to these — they may incur late fees or affect vendor relationships.

The Total A/P row at the bottom shows the total balance across all open bills.

8. FAQ & Troubleshooting

▶
QuestionAnswer
Why don't I see the net position cards? Click "Refresh Live Balances" first. The cards only appear after loading live data from QuickBooks.
Why is CC Outstanding showing $0? Either all cards are paid off, or QB hasn't posted recent charges yet. Check your QB Online credit card accounts to confirm.
Why is CC Expense Breakdown empty? Run the Daily Review sync first (Daily Review page > Sync button). CC purchases need to be synced before categories appear here.
Are CC payments counted as expenses? No. Only actual Purchase transactions count. Transfers from bank to CC are excluded. The CC balance already reflects all payments.
What's the difference between "Refresh Live Balances" and "Sync Payments"? "Refresh Live Balances" pulls real-time account balances and bills from QB. "Sync Payments" syncs payment/invoice history for the YTD analysis below.
How often should I refresh? Whenever you want a current snapshot. There's no automatic schedule — it's on-demand because it queries QB live.
Why do I see very old bills in A/P? QB reports all bills with a balance > $0. Some may be legacy items that were never closed. Contact the accountant to write off or close old bills in QB.
The refresh button shows an error This usually means the QuickBooks token has expired. Contact the system administrator to refresh the QB Online token.
