Travel Expenses + Customer Recipients

April 2026 release — parse receipts, bill them through, route emails per customer

Epic C Customer Recipients

What changed

Email routing for invoices, weekly reports, review portal links, and supplementals is now explicit per customer. The old single customers.email field still works as a fallback, but you can now set:

  • Deliver To — the primary recipient (e.g. Dave Myers at CertiDry when MIT bills the intermediary)
  • CC / BCC — extra copies
  • Internal CC — overrides the default Sharon + David internal CC for this customer

Each recipient has an Applies to list so the same person can be on all flows (unified) or only some (split routing).

Where to manage it

Nav bar → Recipients tab (new). Pick a customer, click Add recipient, set role + applies_to, save.

When a customer has a parent in QuickBooks (QB ParentRef), the parent's info auto-populates under Bill To — that's what prints on the invoice document. The emails go wherever you configure.

Markup toggle

On the customer detail card, the checkbox MIT applies markup determines whether the travel receipt reviewer defaults to applying MIT's markup or billing at cost. Uncheck when a downstream intermediary (like CertiDry) adds their own markup.

Example: CertiDry → NewLand

  1. Customer row is Newland.Urbanite.CertiDry. In QB, its parent is CertiDry.
  2. Auto-populated fields: qb_parent_customer_id, bill_to_cache.name = "CertiDry", bill_to_cache.end_client_name = "Newland".
  3. On the Recipients page, add Dave Myers as Deliver To — applies to invoice + report + review + supplemental.
  4. Uncheck MIT applies markup (CertiDry handles their own overhead).
  5. Email subject will read: Invoice 2026-04-23 — NewLand Urbanite, in care of CertiDry; body opens with in care of CertiDry on behalf of the client NewLand.

Epic R Travel Expenses (MITC platform)

What it does

Snap a receipt — hotel folio, plane, car rental, meal, dry cleaning, parking, gas, rideshare — and the MITC platform parses every line, splits lodging from meals from parking, checks the math, and lets you confirm before clearing to QuickBooks.

Workflow

  1. Open MITC platform → Travel Expenses.
  2. Take photos of every page. "Add page" until the full receipt (all taxes, fees, tips) is captured. On mobile, the Take photo button invokes the native camera.
  3. Pick type (or leave auto-detect) and add project hints like NewLand.Urbanite.CertiDry so the customer matcher can pre-fill.
  4. Submit & parse. The system calls Claude vision, extracts every line, runs a foot check (line sum must equal the stated total within $0.50), and jumps you to the review page.
  5. Review the day rollup — lines grouped by date with subtotals. Verify no pages are missing.
  6. Make the markup decision. The "Clear to QuickBooks" button is disabled until you click either Bill at cost or Apply markup (default 30%, editable). This prevents accidental clears without a deliberate decision.
  7. Clear. Creates a QB Purchase with each line as a Billable line against the customer, MarkupInfo set per line. The Purchase surfaces on the customer's next invoice in QB.

Amendments

If you need to change markup on a receipt that's already cleared, click Amend cleared receipt, enter a required reason, and confirm. The system:

  • Updates the QB Purchase using its stored SyncToken.
  • Logs the before/after state in expense_amendments with who, when, why.
  • If the Purchase is already on a sent invoice, the billing system issues a revised invoice (blue banner, not orange — this is a correction, not a surprise).

There is no month-end lock. Any amend is allowed at any time; the audit trail enforces integrity.

Foot check

The parser computes line_sum (excluding payment lines) and compares it to the stated_total printed on the receipt. Within $0.50 = green "Foot check OK". Outside that = amber warning. A mismatch doesn't block you from clearing, but you should investigate before doing so — usually a missing page.

Review Duplicate detection & out-of-range checks

Before you clear a receipt to QuickBooks, the review page runs four automatic checks and shows them as colored banners at the top. Each banner has its own action:

Red — Duplicate detected (blocks Clear)

The same folio was already posted as another QB Purchase for this customer. The Clear button is disabled until you check a box confirming it’s not actually a duplicate. Examples: same total + same date range + same customer, OR the QB Purchase’s private note references this receipt ID.

What to do: Click the matching Purchase # link, confirm it’s the same receipt, and either skip this upload (it’s already done) or check the override box if you’re intentionally re-running it.

Amber — Overlap with existing Purchase (doesn’t block)

Same customer + same day + same category on an existing un-billed QB Purchase. Two shapes:

  • Itemized vs itemized: totals match within 2% — almost certainly the same day twice.
  • Lump vs itemized: existing estimate of roughly the same amount — you probably want to replace the estimate with actuals.

What to do: Review the highlighted match, decide whether to keep both or skip the overlapping day.

Blue — Already on a sent/paid invoice

This customer has an invoice that was sent (or paid) with a line mentioning travel in the same period. Clearing this receipt adds billable expense on top of what was already invoiced. That’s usually not what you want.

What to do: Open the prior invoice, compare amounts, then either create a supplemental invoice for the delta only, or skip clearing and absorb the cost.

Yellow — Out-of-range amount (acknowledge to proceed)

A day’s category total is at least 2× this customer’s trailing 90-day average AND over the $300 floor. Not a mistake necessarily — could be a downtown hotel or a work dinner — just worth a second look before the bill goes out.

What to do: Tick the acknowledgement checkbox next to each flagged item. The Clear button stays disabled until all are acked.

Both the 2× threshold and the $300 floor are per-customer on the Recipients page if a specific customer needs tighter or looser tripwires.

Invoice Three presentation modes

Each customer has a default for how their travel expenses print on their invoice, and you can override it per-receipt at Clear time. Here’s what the Hilton folio (30 lines, $1,806.03) looks like in each mode:

Mode Line count What the customer sees When to use
Itemized 29 lines Every room, every tax, every tip, every coffee Clients who require full transparency — accountants, legal matters, government contracts
Daily rollup 14 lines One line per (date, category): 2026-04-18 lodging $292.90, 2026-04-18 parking $40.00, etc. Default for most customers — readable without being overwhelming
Lump 5 lines One line per category: Lodging $1,661.96, Meals $67.20, Parking $80.00, Tips $11.87, Credit -$15.00 Flat-fee or clean-look contracts

No matter the mode, the underlying detail is preserved. If a client asks for the breakdown of a lump line, you can produce the full folio. The mode only controls what prints on the invoice.

Comp display

Loyalty comps (Hilton Honors F&B credit, etc.) default to “separate negative line” for transparency — the comp shows as its own line with a negative amount. Flip to “netted into category” if you’d rather silently reduce the meals total without the comp appearing.

Accessible from the receipt review page (only when comp lines exist) and as a per-customer default on the Recipients page.

Essentials Ready to Bill (manual-add workflow)

Our current QuickBooks plan (Essentials) doesn’t support auto-flowing billable expenses from Purchases onto customer invoices — that’s a Plus-tier feature. Until we upgrade, the Ready to Bill page is the manual companion: it shows cleared receipts grouped by customer so you can copy the totals into QB invoices yourself.

Where to find it

On the Travel Expenses page in MIT Document Platform, click the green Ready to Bill button in the top-right. Direct URL: mit-document-platform.vercel.app/travel-expenses/ready-to-bill

Sharon’s step-by-step

  1. Open Ready to Bill. You’ll see one card per customer with outstanding receipts.
  2. Click a receipt to expand it. Pick the presentation mode you want for this customer’s invoice (itemized / daily rollup / lump).
  3. Click Copy lines to clipboard — the lines copy in tab-separated format that pastes directly into QB’s invoice grid.
  4. In QuickBooks, open (or create) the customer’s invoice. Paste into the Product/Service column area. Each line has a description and amount ready to go.
  5. Back in MIT Document Platform, enter the QB invoice number (e.g. 2026-0047) and hit Mark as billed.
  6. The receipt drops out of the Ready to Bill view. You can always open the full receipt via the link to see the original PDF and audit trail.

If you click “Mark as billed” by mistake

Open the receipt in the review page and tell David — there’s a one-line API call to reverse it (POST /api/receipts/[id]/mark-billed?undo=1). We can add a button for this later if it happens often.

Under the hood (reference)

Epic C routes (WTB)

  • POST /functions/v1/sync-customer-billing-hierarchy — pulls QB ParentRef into qb_parent_customer_id + bill_to_cache. Re-run after adding new customers.
  • /customer-recipients page — manage Deliver To, CC, BCC, Internal CC per customer.
  • Supports dryRun flag for CI: { "dryRun": true }.

Epic R routes (MITC platform)

  • POST /api/receipts/upload — FormData with 1..N files, returns receipt ID.
  • POST /api/receipts/parse — re-parse an existing receipt.
  • GET /api/receipts/[id]/duplicate-check — four-signal check payload.
  • GET /api/receipts/ready-to-bill — Essentials-tier billing queue grouped by customer.
  • POST /api/receipts/[id]/mark-billed — record QB invoice # after manual paste.
  • GET /api/receipts/customers/search?q= — QB customer typeahead.
  • GET /api/receipts/[id] — detail view with lines + pages + amendments.
  • PATCH /api/receipts/[id] — update markup decision or linkage.
  • POST /api/receipts/clear — post to QB (or amend if already cleared).
