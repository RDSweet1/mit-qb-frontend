1. Overview & Collection Sequence

▶

The Accounts Receivable module tracks every open invoice from the moment it is created in QuickBooks through to final payment or attorney referral. It automates the dunning (collection reminder) sequence and records every interaction — emails, phone calls, promises to pay, disputes, and payments — in a cradle-to-grave timeline.

The Dunning Sequence

Once an invoice is created, the system automatically progresses through collection stages based on age:

0
Invoice Sent
Day 0
1
Friendly Reminder
+7 days
2
Grace Expired
+15 days
3
Second Notice
+30 days
4
Final Notice
+45 days
5
Attorney
+60 days
Stages 1–4 fire automatically at 9 AM each day. Stage 5 (attorney referral) generates an internal alert only — no email is sent automatically. Sharon must decide the next step.

How the System Gets Its Data

  • Invoices we create: Automatically tracked when you create invoices via the Invoices tab. Due date is set to the 1st of the following month plus 3 days.
  • Invoices created directly in QuickBooks: Pulled in using the “Sync from QB” button, which reads all open invoices from QuickBooks regardless of origin.
  • Payments: Synced from QuickBooks daily. Invoices paid in QB are automatically marked as paid in our system.
  • Customer emails: Replies from customers to the accounting@ mailbox are automatically captured and linked to the correct invoice timeline.

What Gets Tracked

  • Every automated dunning email sent (with delivery and read receipt status)
  • Every manual email sent from Outlook to a customer
  • Customer replies to collection emails
  • Phone calls logged manually
  • Internal notes added by Sharon or David
  • Promises to pay (date recorded, dunning paused until that date passes)
  • Disputes (reason recorded, assigned to responsible person, dunning paused)
  • Billing holds applied at Stage 2+
  • Payments received

2. Navigating to Accounts Receivable

▶

Finding the AR Page

  1. Log in to the MIT Timesheet system
  2. Click the Accts. Rec. tab in the top navigation bar (it appears between Invoices and Daily Review)
  3. If any invoices are overdue for action today, a red badge shows the count on the tab
The Billing Pipeline banner on the home Dashboard also shows a “Collections Due” count in red when AR action is needed, so you'll see it the moment you log in.

The Four Sub-Tabs

TabWhat It Shows
DashboardAging buckets (1–15, 16–30, 31–60, 60+ days overdue), KPI cards, attorney and dispute alert banners
InvoicesFull searchable list of all open invoices with status badges, aging, and balance. Click any row to open the detail drawer.
QueueTwo sections: “Due Today” (action needed now) and “Upcoming” (due within 7 days). Best view for daily AR work.

  3. Take the appropriate action (automated email will already have fired; you may need to log a call or set a promise-to-pay)
  4. Check the Dashboard tab to confirm aging buckets are improving

3. Syncing from QuickBooks

▶

Why Sync?

Our system creates invoice records automatically when you generate invoices in the Invoices tab. However, if an invoice was created directly in QuickBooks, or if a payment was received in QB that we haven't captured yet, the sync buttons bring everything up to date.

Sync from QB (Full Invoice Sync)

  1. Click the Sync from QB button in the top-right of the AR page
  2. The system queries QuickBooks for all open invoices (any with a remaining balance)
  3. New invoices found in QB are added to our system with collection tracking enabled
  4. Invoices we track that are now fully paid in QB are automatically marked as paid
  5. A summary shows how many invoices were added, updated, and marked paid
Run this sync whenever you suspect QB and the AR system are out of sync — for example, after receiving a payment directly in QB, or after creating an invoice manually in QB outside our app.

Sync Payments

The Sync Payments button updates balance_due figures and payment status for all tracked invoices by querying QuickBooks. This is lighter than the full sync and runs automatically each morning as part of the daily AR automation.

Sync Emails

The Sync Emails button reads the last 90 days of the accounting@ inbox and sent folder to find any customer-related messages not yet in the system. See Section 7 for full details.

Automatic Daily Sync

At 9 AM every day, the system automatically:

  1. Syncs the last 3 days of accounting@ email to capture customer replies
  2. Syncs payments from QuickBooks
  3. Finds invoices with next_action_date ≤ today
  4. Fires the next dunning stage for each (or generates an internal alert at Stage 5)

5. Working an Invoice (Detail Drawer)

▶

Opening the Detail Drawer

Click any invoice row in the Invoices list or Queue tab. A drawer slides in from the right showing everything about that invoice in one place.

Financial Strip

At the top of the drawer, four numbers give the financial status at a glance:

  • Invoice Total — original billed amount
  • Paid — payments received to date
  • Balance Due — what remains outstanding (highlighted in red if overdue)
  • Stage — current dunning stage (0–5)

Status Banners

Below the financial strip, colored banners appear when relevant:

  • Orange — No Email on File: Customer email is missing. An “Add Email” button appears to fix it inline.
  • Blue — Promise to Pay: Shows the date the customer promised to pay. Dunning is paused until this date.
  • Amber — Disputed: Shows the dispute reason. Dunning is paused. A “Resolve Dispute” button allows Sharon to reactivate collection after resolution.

Activity Timeline

The full history of the invoice appears in chronological order, combining:

  • Automated dunning emails sent (with delivery / read / bounced badges)
  • Manual Outlook emails sent to this customer (logged via email sync)
  • Customer replies received (inbound emails from the accounting@ inbox)
  • Phone calls logged manually
  • Internal notes
  • Payments received
  • Dispute and resolution events
  • Promise-to-pay confirmations
The timeline is your complete audit trail. You can see exactly what was sent, when it was delivered, when the customer opened it, and what happened next.

Action Buttons

Six action buttons appear at the top of the drawer. See Section 6 for details on each action.

6. Taking Action

▶

Log Call

Records that you spoke with the customer by phone.

  1. Click Log Call
  2. Enter who you spoke with and a brief summary of the conversation
  3. Click Save — the call appears in the timeline immediately

Add Note

Records an internal note visible only to staff (not sent to customer).

  1. Click Add Note
  2. Type your note
  3. Click Save

Promise to Pay

Use when the customer verbally commits to paying by a specific date.

  1. Click Promise to Pay
  2. Enter the date the customer committed to
  3. Click Save

What happens:

  • Dunning is paused — no automated emails will fire while the promise date is in the future
  • A branded confirmation email is sent to the customer summarizing their commitment
  • The promise date appears as a blue banner in the detail drawer
  • If the date passes without payment, dunning automatically resumes from where it left off
If the customer does not pay by the promised date, the system will resume sending dunning emails the following morning.

Dispute

Use when the customer disputes the invoice amount or the work billed.

  1. Click Dispute
  2. Enter the reason for the dispute
  3. Optionally enter the name of the internal person responsible for resolving it
  4. Click Save

What happens:

  • Invoice status changes to disputed
  • Dunning pauses immediately
  • If an assignee was entered and they have an email on file, they receive an email notification
  • The dispute reason appears as an amber banner in the detail drawer

Resolve Dispute

Use when the dispute has been resolved and billing should resume.

  1. Click Resolve Dispute (appears only when status is disputed)
  2. Enter a brief resolution note
  3. Click Save

What happens:

  • Status returns to unpaid
  • Next action date is recalculated based on the current stage and sequence config
  • Dunning resumes on the next scheduled run

Clear Hold

Removes the billing hold flag from the customer account. Use this after a payment plan is established or after full payment is received.

  1. Click Clear Hold
  2. Confirm the action
Billing holds are set automatically at Stage 2 (15 days overdue). Clearing the hold removes the warning that appears on the Invoices page when generating new invoices for this customer.

Send Next Stage

Manually fires the next dunning email immediately, without waiting for the 9 AM automation.

  1. Click Send Next Stage
  2. Review the stage number and confirm
  3. The email is sent and the timeline updates
Use this sparingly — the automation handles timing automatically. This button is for cases where you need to act immediately (e.g., a customer just called asking for a copy of the collection notice).

Add Email (for missing customer email)

If the customer has no email on file, collection emails cannot be sent. An “Add Email” button appears in the orange warning banner at the top of the detail drawer.

  1. Click Add Email in the orange banner
  2. Enter the customer's email address
  3. Click Save — the email is saved to the customer record and collection can proceed

7. Email Inbox Sync

▶

What Email Sync Does

The email sync function reads the accounting@mitigationconsulting.com inbox and sent items folder, identifies messages to or from known customers, and logs them in the AR activity timeline. This means every email interaction — whether sent by the system or written manually in Outlook — is captured in one place.

What Gets Captured

TypeDirectionExample
Automated dunning emailOutboundStage 1 friendly reminder sent by the system
Manual Outlook emailOutboundSharon emailing a customer directly from Outlook
Customer replyInboundCustomer responding to a collection notice

Automatic vs. Manual Sync

  • Automatic: Runs daily at 9 AM, scanning the last 3 days of mail. Keeps the timeline current without any manual action.
  • Manual: Click Sync Emails on the AR page to scan the last 90 days immediately. Use this if you suspect a recent customer reply was missed.

Deduplication

The system tracks message IDs to prevent the same email from being logged twice. Running the sync multiple times will not create duplicate entries.

Delivery and Read Receipts

For automated dunning emails sent through the system, delivery and read status is tracked:

  • Delivered — Email was accepted by the recipient's mail server
  • Read — The recipient opened the email
  • Bounced — Email could not be delivered. Check the customer's email address.
A Bounced receipt means the customer never received the notice. Update their email address using the “Add Email” action and resend.

The Billing Pipeline banner at the top of the Home Dashboard gives a five-stage snapshot of where work currently stands in the billing cycle, so you can see at a glance what needs attention the moment you log in.

When you generate a monthly invoice preview, a collapsible orange banner appears at the top if any customers have an active billing hold. The banner lists each affected customer and their outstanding balance, so you can decide whether to proceed with invoicing or resolve the hold first.

Each affected customer's invoice card also shows an orange Billing Hold badge for quick identification.

9. FAQ

▶

What happens if a customer calls and says they paid?

First, verify in QuickBooks. If the payment is in QB, click Sync Payments on the AR page and it will be marked paid automatically. If the payment is not yet in QB, log a note in the detail drawer documenting the conversation, and wait for the payment to appear in QB before marking it resolved.

Can I stop collection on an invoice temporarily?

Yes — use Promise to Pay if the customer committed to a date, or Dispute if the invoice is being contested. Both actions pause the dunning sequence immediately.

What happens at Stage 5?

An internal alert email is sent to Sharon and David. No email is automatically sent to the customer. The invoice is flagged for manual review and potential attorney referral. Sharon must decide the next step using the AR detail drawer.

Why is a customer showing a billing hold when they've paid?

Billing holds are cleared manually after resolution. If the customer has paid, click Clear Hold in the detail drawer to remove the hold flag. Then confirm in QuickBooks that the payment has been applied.

A customer replied to a collection email but I don't see it in the timeline.

Click Sync Emails on the AR page. This scans the last 90 days of the accounting@ inbox and will capture any replies that were missed by the automatic daily sync. If the reply still doesn't appear, verify that the customer email address in the system matches the address they replied from.

Can the system send emails to multiple contacts at the same customer?

Currently, the system sends to the single email address on the customer record. If a customer needs a secondary contact, add a note in the detail drawer with the additional email and copy them manually from Outlook until multi-email support is added.

How do I know if a collection email was actually received?

The timeline shows delivery and read receipts for every automated email. A Delivered badge means the server accepted it. A Read badge means the recipient opened it. A Bounced badge means delivery failed — check the email address.

What does the AR Dashboard's “Attorney” alert banner mean?

One or more invoices have reached Stage 5 (60+ days overdue). These appear in a red banner at the top of the Dashboard tab. They require manual decision — click each one to open the detail drawer and decide on next steps, which may include engaging legal counsel.
