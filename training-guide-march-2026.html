1. Overview: What Changed & Why

Three features have been added to the system. Together, they eliminate manual data entry steps and give you more flexibility with invoicing:

Feature Before After
Auto-Promote to QB You had to manually import each time entry into QB Online before invoicing. When entries are approved in our app, they are automatically created as TimeActivity records in QB Online. No manual import needed.
Inline Hours Editing If hours were wrong, you had to go into QB Time/Workforce to correct them. Click the hours on any unlocked entry, type the correct value, hit Save. Changes sync back to QB Online automatically.
Interim Invoicing Invoicing was full calendar month only (e.g., “February 2026”). You can now invoice any date range (e.g., Mar 1–15 for a trial). Remaining time automatically falls into the next invoice.
💡
No workflow is removed. Everything you did before still works the same way. These features add new capabilities on top of the existing system.

2. Auto-Promote to QuickBooks Online

What it does

When you approve time entries (individually or in bulk), the system now automatically creates a matching TimeActivity record in QB Online for each entry. This is the step that previously required you to manually import entries from Workforce into QB before invoicing.

3. Inline Hours & Minutes Editing

What it does

You can now correct the hours and minutes on any unlocked time entry directly from the Time Entries page. When you save, two things happen:

  1. The entry is updated in our database (Supabase)
  2. The change is synced back to QB Online (if the entry has a QB TimeActivity)

Before & After

❌ Before

Hours were read-only in our app. To fix wrong hours, you had to:

  1. Open QB Time / Workforce
  2. Find the entry
  3. Edit it there
  4. Wait for it to sync
  5. Re-sync in our app
✅ After

Click the hours → type the correct value → Save.

That’s it. Changes flow to QB Online automatically.

4. Interim / Partial-Month Invoicing

What it does

The Invoices page now supports two modes:

  • Standard — Full calendar month, exactly as before (e.g., “February 2026”).
  • Interim — Any date range you choose (e.g., March 1–15). Perfect for trials, partial-month engagements, or mid-month billing needs.

The key behavior

✅
Interim invoices and standard invoices work together automatically.

When an interim invoice is created, those time entries are marked as HasBeenBilled in QB Online. When you later generate a standard (full month) invoice for the same month, those already-billed entries are automatically excluded. You never get double-billed entries.

6. FAQ & Troubleshooting

Q: I edited hours but the QB badge says “QB Failed” — are the hours lost?

No. Your edit is saved in our database immediately. The QB sync-back is a separate background step. If it fails, your data is safe — the entry will show the correct hours in our app. The QB Online record may still show old hours until the sync succeeds. You can try again by editing the entry or contacting David.

Q: Can I run multiple interim invoices for the same month?

Yes. Each interim invoice bills only the time entries in its date range that haven’t been billed yet. You could run Mar 1–10, then Mar 11–20, then Mar 21–31 if you wanted to. Or run one interim for the first half and a standard for the full month — the standard will only pick up the unbilled remainder.

Q: What if I accidentally invoice the wrong date range?

The invoices are created in QB Online, so you can void or delete them there as you normally would. The time entries in our system will still be marked HasBeenBilled — David can help reset those if needed.

Q: I changed hours on a locked entry and nothing happened. Why?

Locked entries cannot be edited. The pencil icon only appears on unlocked entries. Click the lock icon to unlock the entry first. If the entry has been sent to a customer, you’ll be asked for a change reason.

Q: Do I still need to import time entries into QB manually?

No. That’s the whole point of auto-promote. When you approve entries in our app, they are automatically created as TimeActivity records in QB Online. The old manual import workflow is no longer necessary.

Q: What does “Interim billing” look like to the customer?

The QB invoice memo says “Interim billing — professional services for 2026-03-01 to 2026-03-15” instead of the usual “Professional services for period...”. This helps the customer understand that this is a partial-month bill and that additional charges for the rest of the month may follow.

Q: Can the auto-send monthly cron create interim invoices?

No. The monthly automated cron (auto-send-invoices) always creates standard full-month invoices. Interim invoices are manual-only — you create them from the Invoices page when needed.
